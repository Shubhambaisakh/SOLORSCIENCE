FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about premium solar engineering, net-metering setup, and investment insurance protection.

Solar installations represent massive long-term capital investments designed to run for 25+ years. During this long lifespan, they are vulnerable to natural disasters (cyclones, earthquakes), fire, thefts, or equipment malfunctions. While most installers walk away after construction, we protect your financial return by bundling Tier-1 engineering with premium insurance coverage. If generation stops due to damage, you are fully covered.

Our comprehensive solar insurance coverage protects your assets against fire, lightning, storms, tempest, floods, accidental breakdown, structural collapse, burglary, civil riots, and equipment damage. We also include options for loss of revenue coverage, compensating you for the electricity generation lost while the plant is undergoing repair.

Yes, absolutely. Our end-to-end EPC mandate covers site surveys, structural stability tests, grid capacity feasibility studies, state DISCOM approvals, net-metering installation, and final plant commissioning. Our in-house team handles all government liaisoning for you.

We work exclusively with Tier-1 components. This includes modules from globally certified manufacturers like Waaree, Adani, Enphase, and Jinko, and top-tier inverters from brands like Sungrow, Growatt, and Solis. Every component comes with standard manufacturing warranties (25 years on panels, 5-10 years on inverters).

For industrial and commercial consumers, the average payback period in India ranges between 3 to 4 years. For residential systems, it is around 4 to 5 years. Given that a quality plant operates for over 25 years, you will enjoy free electricity and protected savings for 20+ years.
